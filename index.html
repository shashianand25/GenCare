What is generic medicine?

Generic medicine refers to a pharmaceutical product that is equivalent to a brand-name drug in terms of dosage, strength, administration, quality, and intended use. The key difference is that generic medicines are typically sold under their chemical name rather than a brand name, and they are often much more affordable than branded versions.

Generic medicines contain the same active ingredient as the branded version, ensuring they have the same therapeutic effect. This means that whether you take a branded drug or its generic equivalent, you can expect the same results in terms of treatment. The main difference lies in the cost, as generics are significantly cheaper due to the lower costs associated with producing and distributing them.

Finally, one benefit of choosing a generic drug is its lower cost. Since generic manufacturers do not need to invest in costly research or marketing, they can sell the medication at a much lower price than branded drugs. This makes generic medicines a more accessible option for patients, especially for chronic conditions that require long-term treatment.

Core Attributes

Affordable Pricing

Generic medicines offer the same therapeutic effects as branded versions, but at a much lower cost.

Same Active Ingredients

Generic medicines contain the same active ingredients as their branded counterparts, ensuring they deliver the same quality.

Regulated and Safe

Generic drugs are rigorously tested and must meet the same safety and regulatory standards as branded medicines.

Availability

Generic medicines are widely available in pharmacies.

No Compromise on Quality

Despite being cheaper, generic medicines maintain high manufacturing standards.

Faster Market Entry

Generic versions can quickly enter the market, making medications more accessible.
